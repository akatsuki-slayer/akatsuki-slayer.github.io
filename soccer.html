Soccer

I've been playing soccer since around the age of 5. I remember back in 2nd grade or so, my maximum consecutive juggles was 7. I didn't start playing at the competitive level until around the age of 10. I landed the "around the world (atw)," one of the most basic juggling tricks, in 9th grade. Back then, I practiced juggling a little in my free time, but rarely tricks or dribbling or finishing.

I played on my high school's varsity team all four years and we got second in the state tournament my senior year. I was also named all-conference that year. Up until summer 2021 I could only do the rainbow and inside/outside atw. Since club sports were cancelled my freshman year of college due to COVID, I wanted to get a lot of touches on the ball that summer, before tryouts the next fall. I worked at my neighborhood pool and had a lot of freetime outdoors, so I practiced juggling tricks for about an hour a day all summer long. I learned the crossover, hop the world, neck stall, Homie Touzani around the world (htatw), Touzani around the world (tatw), knee catch, and replay.

I played really well at tryouts and in between drills I showed off the tricks I added to my arsenal over the summer. My friends told me that the guys evaluating us noticed and jotted stuff down on their clipboards. After three consecutive cuts I ended up making the team; the acceptance rate at tryouts was roughly 15%. I'm now currently serving as the secretary and one of my responsibilities is running our Instagram account. Feel free to drop a follow.
