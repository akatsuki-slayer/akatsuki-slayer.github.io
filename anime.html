Anime

My mom's from Japan, so as a kid I watched Studio Ghibli films like Princess Monoke, Spirited Away, and Totoro. Side note: I rewatched these films in winter of 2021 and the first two were so good! Totoro was kind of a let down for me as a young adult because there wasn't much of a plot, but I could see how kids like the cuddly characters. Anyways, I watched American TV (Spongebob was my favorite show at the time), but I never got into anime. Even when I visited Japan as a kid, I would watch the shows I had heard of, like Powderpuff Girls, instead of Naruto.

Fast forward to college and two of my friends were anime fans. Anime fans get a bad rap as being weirdos, so I was surprised. But that summer, when I had a lot of free time I decided I'd give it a try. The first show I watched was Demon Slayer (see the main character, Tanjiro, in the top left). The first episode's tragedy made me tear up and the animation was so well done. Immediately, I texted my friends that this show was INSANE. I ended up watching that show with my mom, so I started watching Naruto while I was at work, at my neighborhood pool. Since then, I've been hooked on anime and that's most of what I watch in my free time. I've always liked animated shows (i.e., Regular Show and Rick and Morty) and I'm Japanese-American so it makes sense why I like it so much. I've also been pressuring my other friends to at least give the medium a go.

So I've been asking my friends and looking at different rankings online (e.g., JW Magazine was the first one I looked at) to find the best anime to watch next. After persevering through over 500 episodes of Naruto, my strategy was to start with classics and modern mega-hits that were also not 100s of episodes long (i.e., not One Piece and Bleach). So without further ado, here are my anime lists. Note that the list of anime I've watched does not count any series which I started, but didn't get very far into (e.g., Clannad).
