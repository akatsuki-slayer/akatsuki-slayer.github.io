Hi, I'm Miles!

I'm from Woodbury, MN (suburb of St. Paul). I'm a rising junior at Vanderbilt University, double majoring in chemical engineering and applied math. I am also minoring in computer science, physics, and chemistry. My goal is to become a software engineer. I made this decision at the beginning of this summer (2022), so I have been practicing LeetCode problems and learning about web development these past couple months. Relevant coursework includes data structures, discrete math, intermediate software design, digital systems, and linear algebra.

Independent projects I've worked on include this static personal webpage with links to subpages showcasing my research (more on that later) and some of my other hobbies, and an "Are You Smarter than a 5th Grader?" quiz game. The inspiration for this game came from YouTube video renditions of the original TV series. YouTubers go to college campuses and ask students relatively simple questions and it's really entertaining when the students get them wrong. You can find links to these pages in the nav bar above. This website was made with HTML and CSS, and is hosted on Github Pages. The quiz game was made using ReactJS and CSS and is hosted on Heroku.

I've been doing research in Dr. Jamey Young's lab in the Department of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at Vanderbilt since January 2022 and I'm continuing it over this summer. Our lab focuses on metabolic engineering, and my group (two PhD students and I) focuses on cyanobacteria in particular. I perform cyanobacterial genetic transformation, metabolite extraction and derivatization, GCMS, and GCMS data analysis. I'm also working with another PhD student on a coding side project, involving machine learning, data mining, and other data science techniques. Click the navbar link for more information.

I like strength training and running as well. My hobbies that don't involve physical activity include watching anime, cooking, and keeping up with the news/politics.
